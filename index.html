Intro

I'm just starting out as a data analyst. Almost everything you'll see here is classwork pursuiant to my certification with Thinkful. In this course we covered every major aspect of the analytics process from ETL to descriptive and inferential statistics to visualization and presentation using industry standard tools such as Excel, Tableau, and Python. I do think my work will speak for itself, so please feel free to take a look.

Work

The HR department needs to better understand how the employees are performing so that they can strategize how to effect company policy such that the company's profits can exceed a billion dollars in revenue. Three options were proposed for analysis by upper management. To satisfy the requirements I created a strategic dashboard and an analytical dashboard, as well as a presentaion of my interpretation of the data and suggestions for how to move forward.
HR Compensation Tool

The C-suite wants to know how their company is performing overall. We have data on sales by location and upkeep costs, but not much else to go on. I took a creative route and leveraged the brand affinity of a Lambourgini that was failing to turn a profit from its own rental sales and concocted a strategy based around driving consumer incentive and pitting customers together in competition to win a 12 day rental of that Lambourini.
Lariat Rental Cars

I've worked with pedicabs from both sides of the table for nearly a decade. In a management role, it's important to have the right tools you need to keep track of what's happening in your company. Many off-the-shelf solutions exist, but the organizational qualities of a pedicab shop are unique to say the least and I never found a single tool that covered all of my bases. After learning how to build simple applications in Rails, I set about laying the groudwork for a web-based application to automate and track as much of the management process as possible. It's still in development and isn't much to look at yet, but I'm now working directly with a local pedicab shop to spec out the final version and get it ready to lauch.
Grand Unified Pedicab Shop

About

I've been working with computers since before I could walk and I'm just barely old enough for that mean something. I remember hacking the gameson my old Macintosh Performa to change the sprites and texts. It stands to reason that after nearly 30 years of interaction and self study, I'm finally starting to get the hang of it.

I did go to college immediately after high school, but I only made it about halfway through before the economy collapsed and my funding options dried up. At first I went to work in IT, but never made it past the technical support desk before getting burned out by it. I spent nearly a decade after that riding a pedicab full-time and tinkering with websites in my free time. I got noticed by the pedicab shop owner community and ended up serving in management and consultant roles with multiple businesses. Nobody can ride the bike forever, though, and if I wasn't leaving that industry behind due to the Coronavirus fundamentally changing the way music festivals will operate, my knees had maybe another year left in them anyway.

I also play and compose music. Most recently, I was performing regularly with an Electric Light Orchestra tribute band, playing the role of "the Jeff Lynn you get when you order from Wish."
